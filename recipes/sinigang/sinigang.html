Sinigang

Sinigang is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines, characterized by its delicious sour and savory flavors.

Sinigang is made of a bunch of vegetables such as string beans, water spinach (regular spinach will work as great as water spinach), tomatoes, eggplant, radish. We also put pork which brings out the overall taste of sinigang.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly see notes
  • 1 lb. young tamarind see notes
  • 1 bunch water spinach chopped
  • 8 pieces string beans cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 pieces eggplants sliced
  • 1 piece daikon radish see notes
  • 8 pieces okras
  • 2 pieces tomatoes sliced into wedges
  • 2 pieces long green pepper
  • 1 piece onion sliced into wedges
  • 2 quarts water
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Pour the tamarind broth into a cooking pot. Let it boil and then add the onion, pork belly, and half the amount of the tomatoes.
  2. Skim-off the floating scums, pour 1 tablespoon fish sauce, cover and continue to simmer for 1 hour.
  3. Add daikon radish and eggplants. Cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the long green pepper, string beans, remaining tomatoes, and okra. Cook for 3 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped water spinach stalks and season with fish sauce and ground black pepper. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Put the water spinach leaves. Cover and turn the heat off. Let the residual heat cook the leaves for 3 minutes before serving.
  7. Share and enjoy!
